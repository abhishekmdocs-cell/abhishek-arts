Biography

Abhishek Misra is an interdisciplinary theatre practitioner based in Bangalore with over a decade of experience spanning devised performance, physical theatre, playback, forum theatre, and collaborative facilitation. Trained in Grotowski practice and the Suzuki Method at the University of South Florida, his work is rooted in embodied storytelling, poetic dramaturgy, and communal exploration.

His original works include Encounters — performed at OurTheatre Bangalore and the University of South Florida — and A Moment of Memory, developed in collaboration with Visual Respiration Theatre Company, Bangalore. His most recent creation, Sapna, is a text-based performance loosely drawn from the short narratives of Jorge Luis Borges.

He is currently developing Captive, a devised piece drawing on five stories by Franz Kafka, and exploring works rooted in Nirmal Verma and Dostoevsky. His devising practice draws on text, movement cells, montage, object theatre, partner choreography, and personal narrative as core tools.

Between 2011 and 2013, he worked with YoursTruly Theatre, Bangalore, on Forum Theatre with school and college students, Playback Theatre, and Mushaira — an improvisational theatre form rooted in Hindustani poetry.
